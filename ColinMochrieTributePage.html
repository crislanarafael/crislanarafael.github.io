Colin Mochrie

Comedian Extraordinaire & Sexiest Canadian Alive

"Give Me Liberty or Give Me a Bran Muffin"

Colin's Timeline of Events

  • 1957 - Born in Kilmarnock, East Ayrshire, Scotland. The oldest of three children.
  • 1964 - Moved to a neighborhood outside of Montreal Quebec.
  • 1969 - Moved to Vancouver, British Columbia and attended Killarney Secondary School. Attended the Studio 58 theatre school which began his stint in improv comedy.
  • 1980 - Began work as a member of the Vancouver TheatreSports League. Met fellow improvisor and future Whose Line cast member Ryan Stiles during this time.
  • 1986 - Moved to Toronto and began performing with The Second City comedy troupe in Toronto.
  • 1989 - Married Debra McGrath, and had a child, Kinley, in 1990.
  • 1989 - Auditioned for the British version of Whose Line Is It Anyway?
  • 1998 - Joined Whose Line Is It Anyway? hosted by Drew Carey and appears on every episode from its debut to its finale in 2006. His favourite games on the show were "Scenes From a Hat", and "Whose Line". He's not been a fan of "Hoedown".
  • 1999 - Guess-starred in three episodes of The Drew Carey Show: "She's Gotta Have It" (1999), "Drew Live" (1999), and "Drew Live II" (2000).
  • 2001 - Guest-appearances in This Hour Has 22 Minutes on CBC Television.
  • 2003 - Appeared in print and television advertisements to encourage people to visit Toronto after the SARS outbreak that struck the city.
  • 2003 - Cameo appearance on Corner Gas.
  • 2007 - Hosted part of the Press Correspondents' Dinner with the President.
  • 2011 - Appeared as a regular cast member on Drew Carey's Improv-A-Ganza on GSN.
  • 2012 - Starred in the ABC improv comedy series Trust Us with Your Life.
  • 2013 - Returns as a regular cast member on the newly revived Whose Line is it Anyway?
